That grouping has a very natural theme. My first choice would be:

**The Way: Salvation in Christ Alone**

The progression is coherent:

- **John 14:6** – Jesus identifies Himself as *the Way* to the Father.
- **Acts 4:12** – Salvation is found in no one else.
- **1 John 5:11–12** – Eternal life is in the Son; whoever has the Son has life.
- **Acts 9:2** – The early Church is called *the Way*.
- **Acts 24:14** – Paul confesses that he worships according to *the Way*.
- **Acts 11:26** – The disciples are first called *Christians*.

Other possible section titles, depending on the style you're aiming for:

- **Jesus, the Only Way**
- **The Way of Salvation**
- **Life in the Son**
- **The Way and the Name**
- **Christians: People of the Way**
- **The Church of the Way** (emphasizes the identity of believers)

For a catechetical outline, I'd probably use **"The Way: Salvation in Christ Alone."** It captures both the doctrinal point (Christ alone saves) and the historical identity of the early Church ("the Way").
